The Color Purple

A brief history

Welcome to my site! My favorite color is purple, and many people know that it is associated with royalty, however there is a rich history behind this regal color.

Purple in Nature

Purple can be found in flowers, trees, and even in the sky during a beautiful sunset. Lavender, hydrangeas, irises, and balloon flowers are examples of purple flowers. Jacaranda trees are native to South America and produce beautiful purple blooms in the Spring.


Purple in Art

Purple is used a lot in street art and in famous artwork as well. Georgia O'Keeffe's Purple Petunias and Vincent VanGogh's Irisesare examples of art that incorporates purple and nature specifically flowers. An example of artwork that uses purple and does not include flowers is Patrick Heron's Green and Purple Painting with Blue Disc: May 1960, which is considered abstract art.


Purple in Fashion

Historically, purple has symbolized wealth and power, so it makes sense that only the rich wore it when it first became available in fashion. Today, it is still viewed as regal and classy, and there are many different shades seen in clothing from deep purple to lilac and lavender.
